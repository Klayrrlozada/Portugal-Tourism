Nature and Adventure Tourism in Portugal

Nature and adventure tourism in Portugal focuses on exploring the country’s natural landscapes, national parks, mountains, islands, and outdoor activities. Portugal offers diverse environments including forests, volcanoes, mountains, rivers, and Atlantic islands, making it ideal for adventure travelers.

Tourists visit Portugal for activities such as hiking, surfing, mountain climbing, whale watching, cycling, and nature exploration. These experiences allow visitors to enjoy Portugal’s rich biodiversity and scenic beauty.

Attractions

Peneda-Gerês National Park

Peneda-Gerês is Portugal’s only national park, established in 1971.

It is known for its mountains, waterfalls, forests, and wildlife, including wolves and wild horses.

The park is popular for hiking, camping, and nature exploration.

Madeira Island

Madeira Island is famous for its dramatic cliffs, volcanic mountains, and lush forests.

It has unique levada trails, irrigation channels used today as scenic hiking routes.

The island has been a major destination for nature tourism since the 19th century.

Azores Islands

The Azores are a group of nine volcanic islands in the Atlantic Ocean.

They are famous for crater lakes, geothermal springs, and whale watching.

The islands formed from volcanic activity millions of years ago.

Ria Formosa Natural Park

Ria Formosa Natural Park is a protected lagoon system in southern Portugal.

It includes barrier islands, marshes, and lagoons, supporting many bird species such as flamingos.

The park became protected in 1987 to preserve its biodiversity.
