Pena Palace

Pena Palace, officially the Park and National Palace of Pena, is a 19th-century Romanticist castle crowning the hills of Sintra, Portugal. With its vivid colors, eclectic architecture, and surrounding parkland, it stands as one of Europe’s earliest Romantic palaces and a defining icon of Portuguese cultural heritage.

Origins and design

Built atop the ruins of a 16th-century Hieronymite monastery destroyed in the 1755 earthquake, the palace was envisioned by King Ferdinand II as a royal summer retreat. Construction began in the 1840s and fused Neo-Gothic, Neo-Manueline, Neo-Islamic, and Neo-Renaissance elements, reflecting the Romantic fascination with medieval and exotic motifs. The palace consists of two wings: the restored convent (Old Palace) and the vividly colored New Palace, joined by an elaborate courtyard and ornamental gates.

Interior highlights

Pena’s interiors remain richly furnished, showcasing 19th-century royal taste with painted trompe-l’œil walls, azulejo tiles, and themed rooms such as the Arab and Indian Rooms. Key attractions include the Manueline cloister, chapel with an alabaster altarpiece by Nicolau Chanterenne, and chambers of King Carlos I.

The park and surroundings

Encircling the palace is Pena Park—a 200-hectare Romantic garden landscaped with winding paths, lakes, and exotic plant species from four continents. Notable features include the Valley of Lakes, Queen’s Fern Valley, and the Chalet of the Countess of Edla, built by Ferdinand II and his second wife. The park’s viewpoints, such as Cruz Alta and Alto do Chá, provide vistas over the Serra de Sintra and the Atlantic coast.

Visiting information

Open daily, the park generally operates from 9 a.m. – 7 p.m., and the palace from 9:30 a.m. – 6:30 p.m. Access is via bus 434 from Sintra train station. Visitor capacity is regulated, and advance online ticket purchase is recommended. The site receives around two million visitors annually, drawn by its fairytale architecture and panoramic setting above the town of Sintra.
