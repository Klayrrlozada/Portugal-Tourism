Wine and Gastronomic Tourism in Portugal

Wine and gastronomic tourism in Portugal focuses on exploring the country’s wine regions, traditional cuisine, and culinary culture. Tourists visit Portugal to experience vineyard tours, wine tastings, local food markets, and traditional restaurants.

Portugal is internationally known for its historic wine production and unique regional dishes. The country’s cuisine reflects influences from the Mediterranean diet, Atlantic seafood traditions, and centuries of exploration and trade.

Attractions

Douro Valley

The Douro Valley is one of the oldest wine regions in the world, officially established in 1756.

It is famous for producing Port wine, one of Portugal’s most famous exports.

The region’s terraced vineyards along the Douro River create stunning landscapes.

Pastel de Nata

Pastel de Nata is Portugal’s most famous pastry.

It was first created in the 18th century by monks at Jerónimos Monastery.

It is enjoyed worldwide and is a major attraction for food tourists visiting Lisbon.

Francesinha

Francesinha originated in Porto in the 1950s.

It is a sandwich made with bread, steak, ham, sausage, melted cheese, and spicy tomato beer sauce, often served with fries.

The dish reflects Porto’s rich food culture.

Bacalhau (salted cod)

Bacalhau (salted cod) has been a staple in Portuguese cuisine since the Age of Exploration, when sailors preserved fish with salt.

Portugal is famous for having over 365 bacalhau recipes.

TIt remains one of the most iconic dishes in Portuguese gastronomy.
