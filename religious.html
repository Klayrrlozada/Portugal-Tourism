Religious Tourism in Portugal

Religious tourism in Portugal involves visiting sacred sites such as churches, monasteries, sanctuaries, and pilgrimage destinations that hold religious and historical importance. Portugal is a predominantly Roman Catholic country, and many of its religious landmarks date back to the Middle Ages and the Age of Discoveries.

Every year, millions of pilgrims and tourists visit Portugal to participate in religious festivals, pilgrimages, and spiritual retreats, making religious tourism an important part of the country's cultural heritage.

Attractions

Sanctuary of Fátima

The Sanctuary of Fátima is one of the most important Catholic pilgrimage sites in the world.

It is associated with the 1917 Marian apparitions, when three shepherd children claimed to see the Virgin Mary.

Every year, millions of pilgrims visit, especially on May 13 and October 13.

Braga Cathedral

Braga Cathedral, built in 1089, is the oldest cathedral in Portugal.

It represents several architectural styles including Romanesque, Gothic, and Baroque.

Braga is known as the religious capital of Portugal due to its many churches.

Bom Jesus do Monte

Bom Jesus do Monte is famous for its Baroque staircase with 577 steps leading to a church.

The sanctuary symbolizes the spiritual journey of faith.

It became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2019.

Batalha Monastery

Batalha Monastery was built to commemorate the Portuguese victory in the Battle of Aljubarrota in 1385.

Construction began in 1386 and lasted more than a century.

The monastery is a masterpiece of Portuguese Gothic architecture.

Tourist activities

Pilgrimage walks up the staircase

Visiting the church and gardens

Enjoying panoramic views of Braga
