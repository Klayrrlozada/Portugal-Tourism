Costa da Caparica

Costa da Caparica is a seaside resort town and coastal area located in the municipality of Almada, Portugal. Stretching along the western edge of the Tagus River estuary, it is part of the Lisbon metropolitan region and renowned for its expansive sandy beaches, surf culture, and proximity to the capital city.

Geography and environment

Costa da Caparica faces the Atlantic Ocean and extends southward from the mouth of the Tagus River. Its shoreline consists of wide, golden beaches divided into zones by wooden walkways and beach bars known as barracas. Behind the dunes lies the fossil cliff area (Arriba Fóssil), a geological formation protected as a natural reserve for its ancient sedimentary layers and biodiversity.

Tourism and recreation

The area is a popular summer destination for Lisbon residents and international visitors seeking easy beach access. It hosts numerous surf schools, beach clubs, and seasonal music festivals. During peak months, the beaches are connected by a small tourist train, the Transpraia, which runs several kilometers along the coast linking different bathing zones.

Culture and lifestyle

Traditionally a fishing community, Costa da Caparica retains elements of maritime heritage, including colorful fishing boats and local seafood restaurants. Recent years have seen growth in boutique hotels and modern cafes, attracting younger visitors and digital nomads while preserving its relaxed, local character.

Access and transportation

The area is easily reached from Lisbon by car, ferry, or bus. The 25 de Abril Bridge provides direct road access, while ferries from Cais do Sodré connect to nearby Trafaria and Cacilhas. Public transport and cycling routes support eco-friendly travel to the beaches.
