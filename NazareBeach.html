Nazaré Beach

Nazaré Beach is a coastal destination in Nazaré, Portugal, known for its wide sandy shore and record-breaking waves. It has become world-famous among surfers and spectators for its unique underwater canyon that produces some of the largest surfable waves on Earth.

Geography and natural features

The beach lies along Portugal’s central Atlantic coast and is divided by a rocky headland called Sítio da Nazaré. Offshore, the 5,000-meter-deep Nazaré Canyon funnels Atlantic swells directly toward the coastline, amplifying wave height and power. This rare geological feature gives Praia do Norte its extreme surf conditions, while the southern Praia da Nazaré remains calmer for swimming and family tourism.

Surf culture and records

Since the early 2010s, Nazaré Beach has become a global hub for big-wave surfing. Athletes such as Garrett McNamara, Maya Gabeira, and Rodrigo Koxa have set Guinness World Records there for the largest waves ever surfed. The annual Nazaré Tow Surfing Challenge, organized by the World Surf League, attracts top international surfers each winter.

Tourism and local economy

Beyond surfing, Nazaré Beach retains a traditional Portuguese fishing-village charm, with white-washed houses, seaside restaurants, and vibrant local markets. Visitors can ride the Nazaré Funicular to Sítio for panoramic ocean views. The mix of heritage, seaside leisure, and extreme sports tourism makes Nazaré one of Portugal’s most dynamic coastal attractions.
